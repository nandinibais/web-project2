Overview

The Shreemad Bhagavatam (also called Bhāgavata Purāṇa) is a revered scripture in Hinduism that narrates the divine pastimes of Lord Vishnu, especially Lord Krishna. It is considered one of the greatest texts of Bhakti (devotion) and spiritual wisdom. The text is a dialogue between Sage Śuka (son of Vyasa) and King Parikshit, who listens to it in his final seven days before death. It discusses:

  • Creation of the Universe (Cosmology, Origin of life)
    • Avatars of Lord Vishnu, including Krishna, Rama, Narasimha, and others Stories of great devotees (Prahlada, Dhruva, Ambarisha, etc.) Philosophy of Bhakti Yoga – Pure devotion as the highest path to liberation
Divine Leelas (Pastimes) of Lord Krishna, including his childhood, Govardhan lifting, and Rasleela It is regarded as the “Ripened fruit of the Vedas”, teaching that unconditional love and surrender to God lead to eternal bliss.
