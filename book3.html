Overview

The Uddhav Gita is a profound spiritual discourse found in the 11th Canto of the Shreemad Bhagavatam. It is a farewell message of Lord Krishna to Uddhava, given before Krishna's departure from the mortal world. Unlike the Bhagavad Gita, which was spoken to Arjuna on the battlefield, the Uddhav Gita is more philosophical and intended for advanced seekers of truth. It includes teachings on:

  • Detachment (Vairagya) – Renouncing worldly illusions
  • Bhakti Yoga – The path of pure devotion
  • Jnana Yoga – The knowledge of the eternal soul
  • The Impermanence of the World – Understanding Maya (illusion)
  • The Duties of a Devotee – How to live a spiritual life in a material world
Krishna explains how true liberation comes from transcending desires, practicing devotion, and realizing the Supreme Reality. The Uddhav Gita is sometimes called the “Final Teachings of Lord Krishna” and is revered by seekers of self-realization.
